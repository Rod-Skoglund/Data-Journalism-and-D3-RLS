Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Obesity vs Poverty

There is a general trend between Obesity and Poverty. Th states with higher Poverty rtes tend to be slightly more obese. The states with high Poverty rates and high Obesity rates tend to be in teh Southeastern part of the US.

Obesity vs Average Age

There does not seem to be a correlation between Obesity and Average Age.

Obesity vs Average Household Income

There seems to be a correlation between Obesity and Average Household Income. The lower the average household, the more Obese people tend to be. The states with higher average household incomes and lower Obesity rates tend to be in the northeastern region of the US, but include Hawaii and Washington DC. The states with the lower incomes and higher Obesity rates tend to be in the southeastern region of the US.

Smoking vs Poverty

There is a trend between Smoking and poverty. States with lower Poverty rates tend to have lower Smoking rates. States with high Poverty and high Smoking rates tend to be in the southeastern part of the US.

Smoking vs Average Age

Thhere does not seem be a correlation between Smoking and Average Age. Interesting note - Utah has the lowest Average Age and the lowest Smoking rate of all the states by a fairly large margin.

Smoking vs Average Household Income

States with higher Smoking rates tend to have lower Average Household Income. Once again, states with lower Average Household Income and higher Smoking rates tend to be in the southeastern part of the US.

Lacks Healthcare vs Poverty
